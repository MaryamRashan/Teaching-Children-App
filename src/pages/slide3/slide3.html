Abdullah bin Mas'ud reported:

The Prophet used to supplicate:

اللَّهُمَّ اِنِّي اَسْاَلُكَ الْهُدَى ، وَالتّْقَّى ، والْعَفَافَ ، وَالغِنَىَ

O Allaah! I beseech You for guidance, piety, chastity and contentment.
Sahih Muslim

"Guidance" means guidance towards virtue which one needs at every step. The ability to do good and steadfastness on the Right Path is also covered by the term guidance. To comply with the Orders of Allaah and to prevent oneself from what he has forbidden is Taqwa (fear of Allaah), the importance of which needs no elaboration. 'Affaaf' is prevention from sins.

It also means evasion of seeking help from others. 'Ghina' means riches which make one independent of others so much that all one's hopes are centered on AllaahAlone. The prayer quoted in this Hadith is very comprehensive indeed.
